🗺️ Setting Up the Competition Board Setup Guide

Before every practice run, the board needs to be set up exactly the same way. Consistent setup means consistent results — small differences in model placement can throw off your entire program!

1 Open the board setup guide below and study each mission model's starting position carefully.
2 Place the mat flat on a stable table — no bumps or overhangs on the edges.
3 Build and place each model exactly as shown — check angles, color alignment, and which hole it snaps into.
4 Double-check your Launch area — your robot and all attachments must fit with nothing touching the black border.
5 Have a second person verify the setup before starting a timed run.
⚠️ Important: At competition, referees set up the board. But in practice, always reset to the official starting positions between runs — otherwise your scores won't reflect real competition conditions.

🎤 Robotics Presentation Team Project

The Robotics Presentation is one of the judged segments of competition — you will present your team's robot design process, explain key decisions, and show off what makes your solution unique. Judges are looking for clear communication, teamwork, and evidence of the engineering process.

💡 You have two documents to help you prepare. The first is your team's working presentation guide — use it to plan what each person will say. The second is a reference document with additional details and talking points.
1 Read through both documents below so you know the full scope of the presentation.
2 Decide who will cover which part — every team member should have a speaking role.
3 Practice saying your part out loud at least five times before competition day.
4 Run through the full presentation as a team during the Apr 9 Mock Competition meeting.

💬 Official Q&A from the CA Southern Spring Showdown Confirmed Rules

These are real questions submitted by teams — and answered by the competition admins. Read every answer carefully: they clarify rules that are easy to misread in the rulebook.

🏁 Competition Format
How many matches will each team get on competition day?
1 Practice round, 3 performance rounds, 2 On The Spot rounds.
🤖 Robot Starting Position
Can we run our robot from the black home area to do a mission, or does it have to start from the other home area?
All robots must start from the Launch area — the 2 quarter circles on the sides of the field. All rules from the Fall still hold unless expressly stated.
⏱️ Scoring
If we complete a mission but it gets undone during the run, does it still count?
All scoring is done at the end of the match — if something gets undone, it will not count.
🤝 On-The-Spot — Alliances
Does "random draw" mean our whole team pairs up with another team, or are individual members mixed into new alliances?
Random draw will be whole teams partnering up with other teams.
During preparation, is the whole team allowed to collaborate, or only the selected operators?
All team members are involved in the planning and practice process. We will restrict the number of operators at the table, but that doesn't exclude the others from participating.
🔢 Max Score
What is the maximum possible score?
The max score is 360 points.
🤖 Robot Design
Is a small robot (e.g., Driving Base 1 Lego Spike) allowed at competition?
You can use any Spike Prime robot design. You cannot use Spike Prime Essentials though.
