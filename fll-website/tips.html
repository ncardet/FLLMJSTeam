💡 Expert Knowledge

Tips & Tricks

Wisdom from experienced FLL teams. Master these strategies to dominate Robotic Jones & The Temple of LEGO.

🗺️ The Big Picture

General Strategy

🎯

Plan Your Run Route

  • Map out which missions your robot will attempt before every practice run — don't just wing it.
  • Group nearby missions into a single trip to reduce travel time.
  • Identify your "anchor" missions (easy, reliable points) and always do those first.
  • Use the Score Calculator to figure out the highest-value combination of missions.
  • Have a Plan A and a Plan B in case something goes wrong mid-run.
💡 The team that scores the most doesn't always attempt the most missions — they attempt the right missions consistently.
🔋

Preserve Your Supply Caches

  • Mission 15 (Supplies Caches) is worth up to 50 points — almost as much as 5 normal missions!
  • Every interruption outside of home costs a cache. Only touch the robot when it's in the home zone.
  • If your robot gets stuck mid-run, resist the temptation to grab it immediately — wait to see if it recovers.
  • Practice until your robot rarely needs to be interrupted.
⚠️ Never interrupt the robot outside home unless absolutely necessary. One interruption = 10 points lost!
⏱️

Time Management

  • You have exactly 150 seconds. Time every practice run with a stopwatch.
  • Know the time each mission segment takes — cut ones that consistently eat up too much time.
  • Factor in travel time between missions — it adds up fast.
  • Aim to finish with 10-20 seconds to spare so you're not rushing the last mission.
  • Simpler missions at the end of the run are safer when you're short on time.
💡 A robot that completes 6 missions perfectly scores more than one that attempts 12 and fails half.
🔄

Consistency is King

  • Aim for the same score 10 runs in a row, not one perfect run.
  • A mission that succeeds 8 out of 10 times is more valuable than one that succeeds 3 out of 10.
  • Log your scores from every practice run to track improvement over time.
  • When something breaks, fix it — don't band-aid over problems.
📋

Pre-Match Checklist

  • Verify your robot and attachments fit completely in the launch area.
  • Check that robot height is under 12 inches (305 mm).
  • Charge your battery fully — bring a spare if allowed.
  • Test your first two missions in the practice area before your match.
  • Have one team member assigned to watch the clock and call out time warnings.
  • Double-check all attachment connections — things come loose in transit!
⚠️ Mission 00 (Equipment Inspection) is free 20 points — never skip the height check!
🤝

Teamwork & Roles

  • Assign clear roles: Driver (touches the robot), Navigator (calls out the plan), Timer.
  • The driver should stay focused on the field — the navigator communicates with referees and tracks time.
  • Practice transitions between your team's sub-runs so there's no hesitation.
  • Celebrate small wins during practice — positivity builds better teams.

🤖 Build Smarter

Robot Design Tips

🏗️

Base Robot Design

  • Build a compact, low base — staying under 12 inches is non-negotiable for Mission 00.
  • Use a sturdy, symmetrical drive base. Wobbly robots drift off course.
  • Keep your center of gravity low and centered to prevent tipping.
  • Use the same wheel type on both sides and ensure they're balanced.
  • Protect motors and the hub from accidental bumps to mission elements.
🔧

Attachments & Tools

  • Design attachments that are quick to swap — use simple push-on connectors.
  • One good attachment that handles multiple missions beats five fragile single-purpose ones.
  • Test each attachment at least 20 times before competition.
  • Color-code attachments so the team can swap them fast without confusion.
  • Always reinforce attachment connection points — they're the most common failure spot.
💡 The fewer attachments you need to swap mid-run, the more time you save.
📐

Navigation & Alignment

  • Use wall-following techniques at the start of each sub-run for consistent alignment.
  • Add physical guides or "jig" pieces to help the robot align precisely with mission elements.
  • Mark your launch position on the robot and the field — consistency starts at home.
  • Gyro sensors are powerful for straight-line movement — calibrate before every match.
  • Slow down before critical moments — speed is useless if the robot overshoots.
🔌

SPIKE Prime Best Practices

  • Always start programs with the robot battery above 80% charge.
  • Calibrate the gyro sensor at room temperature — it can drift if it gets warm.
  • Use wait_for_seconds carefully — it can cause timing issues. Prefer encoder-based movement.
  • Test programs after any hardware changes — small builds can alter motor performance.
  • Save backup versions of programs before making large changes.
📋 Official Q&A
Is a small robot (e.g., Driving Base 1 Lego Spike) allowed at competition?
You can use any Spike Prime robot design. You cannot use Spike Prime Essentials though.

🗿 Mission by Mission

Mission-Specific Tips

Targeted advice for each of the 16 missions. Study the ones your robot targets most!

🔍

M00 · Equipment Inspection

  • Measure your robot's height with all attachments loaded before the inspection — not after.
  • Keep a 12-inch ruler at your team table as a quick reference.
  • If you're borderline, build slightly shorter — referees measure from the mat.
  • This is 20 free points — there is no excuse for failing this!
💡 Value: 20 pts — Always achievable. Never miss this one.
🛒

M01 · The Great Escape

  • Approach the mine cart at a consistent angle — test the approach multiple times.
  • A gentle push is often better than a fast collision — avoid knocking the cart off the track.
  • Aim for both points: cart through the arch and still on the track (20 pts total).
  • If staying on the track is hard, at least secure the 10 pts for going through the arch.
💡 Value: up to 20 pts. A great "on the way" mission if the path goes near it.
📋 Official Q&A
The instructions say "push." Must it be pushed, or can it be pulled or the track lifted?
You can accomplish the mission in any legal way.
🪨

M02 · Restore the Cave

  • The cave walls must stay upright — slow and gentle is the approach here.
  • Use a funnel-shaped attachment to guide the boulder into the cave without touching walls.
  • Practice positioning the boulder on the orange circle for the bonus 10 points.
  • If the bonus is unreliable, still collect the 20 pts for boulder in cave with walls up.
💡 Value: up to 30 pts. High-value mission — prioritize if nearby other missions.
📋 Official Q&A
Are there any points if the boulder is inside the cave but the walls are knocked down?
No, the walls must be up to score points.
🚩

M03 · Trailblazers

  • Markers must not be touching the mat — use a gripper that places them precisely on the mountain.
  • Placing 2 markers = 20 pts. Both markers count — don't combine them!
  • Practice dropping markers at the exact height so they land on the mountain, not the mat.
  • This mission pairs well with others that send the robot to that side of the field.
💡 Value: up to 20 pts. Often overlooked — easy points for a well-placed arm.
🏺

M04 · Replace the Relic

  • This is a high-complexity, high-reward mission (up to 40 pts). Invest practice time here!
  • Getting the idol in the center of the pedestal (30 pts) requires precision — use gyro navigation.
  • Even if centering fails, idol on pedestal (20 pts) + relic removed (10 pts) = 30 pts — still excellent.
  • Build an attachment that can hold the idol securely and release it with control.
  • Practice the approach direction — even 1 cm off can drop you to a lower tier.
⚠️ Idol tier points are exclusive — only the highest achieved counts.
🪔

M05 · Light the Way

  • The lantern must be standing in the oval — lying down doesn't count.
  • For the 5-pt bonus, ensure your attachment leaves the lantern facing the panel.
  • A slow, controlled placement is better than a fast push that topples the lantern.
  • Practice from multiple approach angles to find the most reliable one.
💡 Value: up to 15 pts. Quick mission if you have the right attachment.
🫙

M06 · Hide the Relics

  • The table must tip completely over — partially tipped doesn't score.
  • Hit the table with enough force from the right angle to ensure it goes all the way over.
  • Test the minimum force needed — too hard can cause other problems.
  • This is a simple push mission — great for a fast, efficient sub-run.
💡 Value: 10 pts. Easy win if your path passes the table.
💨

M07 · Boulder Dash

  • Release all boulders from the ramp first (10 pts), then try to collect them into the quarry.
  • Each boulder in the quarry is 5 pts — getting all 3 = 15 more pts on top of the 10 pt release.
  • Boulders cannot be attached together — your collector needs to handle them separately.
  • Test how far boulders roll when released — use that to your advantage.
💡 Value: up to 25 pts. Release alone (10 pts) is easy; quarry placement requires practice.
🌉

M08 · Bridge the Gap

  • The bridge must be completely lowered — ensure your robot pushes it all the way down.
  • Approach from the correct side — check the field setup carefully.
  • A simple pushing attachment works well here. No need to over-engineer.
  • Pair this with nearby missions for an efficient route.
💡 Value: 20 pts. Straightforward if approached correctly — great ROI on time.
🔦

M09 · Reflect the Light

  • Lower the grey reflective panel completely to score the 15 points.
  • Design an attachment that can reliably engage and lower the panel.
  • Check the field mat to understand the panel orientation before your run.
  • Pairs well with Mission 05 (Light the Way) since both involve the lantern area.
💡 Value: 15 pts. Often achievable in the same trip as Mission 05.
🟩

M10 · Claim Territory

  • Markers must land on green panels or in the grey divot — not on the mat.
  • Only 2 markers count total — one per dig site. Don't waste time placing a third!
  • The grey divot is the small recessed/trapezoid-shaped area on the grey model.
  • Markers can tip over and still count — they just need to be completely on the divot or panel.
  • Practice marker placement accuracy — dropping on the mat scores nothing.
💡 Value: up to 20 pts (2 markers × 10 pts, one at each dig site).
📋 Official Q&A
Can we put all the site markers on one dig site?
Only two markers will count, and there needs to be one at each dig site.
Does the marker have to be standing on the divot, or can it tip over and still count?
As long as it is completely on the divot, it will count.
🗿

M11 · Retrieve the Lost LEGOlith

  • Both walls standing = 25 pts — this is the goal! A gentle, slow approach is essential.
  • If walls fall, 15 pts (one wall) or 10 pts (no walls) still score — don't give up if a wall falls.
  • Practice a slow entry into the room — speed is your enemy here.
  • Design an attachment that can grab or push the LEGOlith into home reliably.
  • Test what "disturbs" the walls in practice so you can avoid those movements.
💡 Value: up to 25 pts. One of the highest-value missions — worth significant practice time.
🚪

M12 · Reveal the Secret Passage

  • The passage must be completely uncovered — move the barrier fully out of the way.
  • Know which direction the barrier needs to move and approach accordingly.
  • A hook or pusher attachment works well to slide the barrier aside.
  • This mission is near Mission 10 (Claim Territory) — try to combo them.
💡 Value: 20 pts. Simple mechanism — great value for a reliable push.
⛵

M13 · Looting

  • Lower the treasure ship completely — partial lowering scores nothing.
  • Understand the ship's hinge direction — come from the right side.
  • A consistent pushing attachment that engages the ship at the right height works well.
  • This is a clean all-or-nothing mission: 20 pts or 0 pts. Make it reliable.
💡 Value: 20 pts. Great mission if you're already traveling to that side of the field.
⚙️

M14 · Disarm the Trap

  • Each gear removed = 5 points, max 3 gears = 15 points total.
  • Study how the gears are positioned — a scraping or pulling attachment can remove multiple in one pass.
  • Even removing 1 gear is 5 points — this mission has low barriers to entry.
  • Practice extracting gears without tipping or disturbing nearby mission elements.
💡 Value: up to 15 pts. Reliable partial scoring makes this a great consistent mission.
🔴

M15 · Supplies Caches

  • The ultimate passive mission — earn up to 50 pts just by not interrupting your robot outside home.
  • Plan your programs to be fully autonomous. Every human touch outside home = −10 pts.
  • If your robot gets stuck, resist helping it until it re-enters home or time runs out.
  • 5 or 6 caches = same 50 pts — so one touch isn't catastrophic, but two is very costly.
  • This mission should guide how you design your entire strategy.
⚠️ This is the single most important mission to plan around. 50 pts for doing nothing wrong!

🏆 Game Day

Competition Day Wisdom

😌

Stay Calm & Trust Your Practice

  • Nerves are normal — take a deep breath before each match.
  • Trust your robot. You've practiced this. Stick to the plan.
  • Don't change your strategy at the last minute unless you have a very good reason.
  • If something goes wrong in Match 1, reset, debrief quickly, and come back stronger in Match 2.
📦

What to Bring

  • Fully charged battery (and a backup if allowed)
  • Extra LEGO pieces for quick repairs
  • Ruler to double-check the 12-inch height
  • A printed copy of the mission rules for quick reference
  • Stopwatch or timer app
  • Team spirit and a positive attitude!
🔬

Use Practice Rounds Wisely

  • Use the practice field to confirm your robot is running as expected — not to try new things.
  • Check that the competition field's elements are positioned as expected (referees set them up).
  • Note any differences from your practice field and make quick mental adjustments.
  • Watch other teams at the practice field — you might spot something useful.
🤠

The Indiana Jones Mindset

  • "I'm making this up as I go." — Even if something goes wrong, improvise and keep going.
  • Every explorer hits obstacles. The best ones adapt on the fly.
  • Win or lose, celebrate what your team has built and learned together.
  • Gracious Professionalism: cheer for other teams. We're all adventurers on the same quest.
💡 "Fortune and glory, kid. Fortune and glory." — But most importantly, have fun!
